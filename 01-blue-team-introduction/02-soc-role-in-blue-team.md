## Objective
Understand how the SOC fits within an organization, the role of the Blue Team, and how SOC analysts progress in their careers.

---

## Key Concepts

### Security Team Structure
Organizations organize cybersecurity roles in a hierarchy:

- Executives → Business decisions  
- CISO → Leads cybersecurity strategy  
- Security Managers → Oversee teams  
- Technical Teams (Blue Team) → Handle daily security operations  

✅ Key Idea:  
The **CISO is responsible for major security decisions** within an organization. 【1-3cb658】  

---

### What is the Blue Team?
The Blue Team focuses on **defensive security**, including:

- Monitoring systems  
- Investigating alerts  
- Responding to threats  

✅ Key Idea:  
Blue Team = defense, while Red Team focuses on offensive security. 【2-724a36】  

---

### SOC Roles

The Security Operations Center (SOC) includes different levels of analysts:

- **L1 Analyst (Junior)** → Handles alert triage  
- **L2 Analyst** → Investigates complex threats  
- **L3 Analyst** → Advanced analysis and threat hunting  

✅ Key Idea:  
Each level builds on the previous—L1 focuses on detection, while higher levels handle deeper investigations. 【3-114f8b】  

---

### Incident Response

- SOC analysts detect and triage threats  
- Serious incidents are handled by the **CIRT (Incident Response Team)**  

✅ Key Idea:  
SOC detects → CIRT responds  

---

### Career Progression

Typical SOC career path:

L1 Analyst → L2 Analyst → L3 Analyst → Specialized roles  

Additional concept:
- **MSSP (Managed Security Service Provider)** = outsourced SOC services  

---

## Takeaways

- The SOC is part of a larger cybersecurity structure  
- Blue Team focuses on defense and threat detection  
- Security roles are specialized and collaborative  
- SOC analysts follow a clear career progression
