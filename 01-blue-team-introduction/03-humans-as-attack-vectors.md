# Humans as Attack Vectors

## Room Link
https://tryhackme.com/room/humansattackvectors

---

## Objective
Understand how attackers exploit human behavior to gain unauthorized access to systems and information.

---

## Key Concepts

### What Are Human Attack Vectors?
Human attack vectors involve **manipulating people rather than systems** to gain access.

✅ Key Idea:  
Humans are often the **weakest link in security**

---

### Social Engineering

Social engineering is the primary method used in human-based attacks.

Common techniques include:
- **Phishing** → Fake emails to steal credentials  
- **Vishing** → Voice calls pretending to be legitimate  
- **Smishing** → Malicious text messages  
- **Pretexting** → Creating a fake scenario to gain trust  

---

### Phishing Attacks

Phishing is one of the most common attack methods.

Attackers:
- Impersonate trusted entities (banks, companies, IT support)  
- Trick users into clicking links or entering credentials  

✅ Key Idea:  
Always verify the source before interacting with links or attachments  

---

### Insider Threats

Threats can also come from **inside an organization**:

- Malicious employees  
- Negligent users  
- Compromised accounts  

✅ Key Idea:  
Not all threats come from external attackers  

---

### Security Awareness

Organizations defend against human attacks by:

- Employee training  
- Awareness programs  
- Phishing simulations  
- Clear security policies  

✅ Key Idea:  
Educating users is one of the most effective defenses  

---

## Takeaways

- Many attacks don’t require technical exploits, just human error  
- Social engineering is widely used in real-world attacks  
- Security depends on both technology and user awareness  
- Recognizing suspicious behavior is critical for prevention
